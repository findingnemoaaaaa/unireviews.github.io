One Punch Man


In a supercontinent version of Earth with four Moons, powerful monsters and supervillains wreak havoc. The Hero Association, created by millionaire Agoni, employs superheroes to fight evil. Saitama, an unassociated hero from City Z, trains to defeat enemies with a single punch, leaving him bored. He becomes a mentor to Genos, a cyborg seeking revenge against another cyborg who killed his family and destroyed his hometown. Saitama and Genos join the Hero Association, but Genos gains an S-Class rank, while Saitama becomes a C-Class hero.

The world is in danger as alien Boros invades the planet. The heroes learn that the Monster Association, an organization of monsters under City Z, is intent on destroying the Hero Association. They attack cities, recruit fighters, and kidnap a Hero Association executive's son. Garo, a former apprentice of the hero Bang, terrorizes heroes due to childhood bullying. The Hero Association assaults the headquarters, causing a battle that destroys City Z and kills almost every monster.

Saitama meets the highest-ranked hero Blast, who warns him of a threat called "God." Garo receives some of "God's" power, causing him to kill all heroes, including Genos. Saitama goes back in time and subdues Garo before killing the heroes. Genos, who reconnected with his core from the original timeline bought by Saitama, remembers the events of the original ominous future caused by Garo. With the Monster Association destroyed, Saitama gets promoted to A-Class and relocates to the Hero Association's HQ in A-City.

Comment:

"One Punch Man" is a one-of-a-kind combination of action and humor that parodies the superhero genre while also dissecting established stereotypes. The wit is razor-sharp, while the series explores deeper issues and character development. Saitama, a strong protagonist, digs into his own hardships in order to get respect. Supporting characters such as Genos, as well as the many heroes and villains, bring depth and complexity to the story. The series explores the concept of heroism by showing faults and self-interest within the hero group, causing viewers to rethink their own views of heroism.
