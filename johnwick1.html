John Wick


John Wick, grieving the death of his wife Helen, receives a beagle puppy to help him cope. Russian gangsters, led by Iosef Tarasov, break into John's home, assault him, kill the puppy, and steal the car. Iosef is the son of Viggo Tarasov, the boss of New York City's Russian mafia. Viggo berates Iosef for incurring John's wrath, revealing John was once a hitman and feared in the criminal underworld. John recovers a concealed stash from his former career and refuses Viggo's attempt to make amends for his son's actions.

John lodges at the Continental, a luxurious hotel where criminal activities are forbidden. John is attacked by Viggo's henchman Kirill and is forced to retreat to the Continental for medical attention. At the church, John destroys Viggo's cache of cash and extensive blackmail material. John confronts Viggo and kills Kirill, and Marcus intervenes to save him. Perkins witnesses their meeting and reveals Marcus's duplicity to Viggo.

John races to New York Harbor, where he fights and mortally wounds Viggo. Resigned to dying from his injuries, John watches a video of Helen telling him they need to go home.

Comment:

"John Wick" excels in action choreography and stunt work, featuring expertly crafted fight sequences and Keanu Reeves' dedication to the character. The film introduces a secret society of assassins with unique underworld rules, and the Continental Hotel serves as a hub for memorable characters. The film's mythology is gradually unveiled, leaving room for future franchise exploration.
