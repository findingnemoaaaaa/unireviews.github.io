Home Alone 3


In this story, four wanted criminals, Peter Beaupre, Alice Ribbons, Burton Jernigan, and Earl Unger, steal a $10 million missile-cloaking microchip from a remote control toy car in Silicon Valley, California. They steal the chip and search every house in Mrs. Hess's suburban neighborhood. Alex Pruitt, given the remote control car by Mrs. Hess, discovers the stolen chip and informs the U.S. Air Force Recruitment Center. The thieves break into the Pruitts' house, rig it with booby traps and suffer injuries.

Alex flees and rescues Mrs. Hess, who has been duct taped to her garage by Ribbons. Beaupre ambushes Alex, but the latter uses a bubble gun to scare him off. FBI agents and Chicago PD officers arrive at Alex's siblings' school, arresting them. Beaupre hides in a snow fort, and Stan's parrot threatens to light fireworks. Beaupre is discovered and arrested.

Later, the Pruitts, Hess, and authorities celebrate Alex's birthday as the house is being repaired. The thieves are shown to have contracted Alex's chickenpox during their mugshots.

Comment:

"Home Alone 3" lacks the original cast, particularly Macaulay Culkin as Kevin McCallister, and lacks the charm and charisma of previous films. The new characters lack audience connection, and the comedic timing and chemistry between actors are weak, resulting in less memorable and impactful performances. While it may provide mild entertainment for younger viewers, it pales in comparison to beloved classics.
