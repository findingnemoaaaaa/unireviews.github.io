Mouse


The mother of the victim child, who attended the trial, expressed her desire for the defendant to know the value of their youngest child. However, the defendant's response was not sorry or guilty, but rather, it was the hardest thing for him to not see cherry blossoms. This response was met with shock and fury, as the defendant was born with a different gene, the "Psychopathic Gene." Psychopaths have the same brain as ordinary humans but lack a mirror neuron, unable to feel the pain of others or perceive feelings like guilt, sympathy, compassion, or regret. This lack of empathy and remorse leads to unbearable rage. The scriptwriter aimed to make the drama against this backdrop, aiming to make psychopath murderers feel the pain, reflect on their actions, and atone. The scriptwriter hoped that the defendant would understand their sins sincerely and feel remorse, which would be the best punishment and salvation for the psychopathic murderers.

Comment:

"Mouse" is a captivating series that builds tension and suspense, leaving viewers eager for the next twist. The intricate plot raises questions about morality, nature vs. nurture, and evil. The exceptional performances of Lee Seung-gi and Lee Hee-joon, as Jung Ba-reum and Go Moo-chi, add depth to their complex relationship.
