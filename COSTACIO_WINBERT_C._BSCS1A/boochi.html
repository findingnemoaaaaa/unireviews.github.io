Bocchi The Rock!


Extremely anxious and socially awkward Hitori Gotō longs to become a rock musician in spite of her struggles, whilst fulfilling her desires to one day make friends. She is suddenly given a chance to do so after she is taken in by Nijika Ijichi to become a member of her newly formed Kessoku Band.

Yearning to make friends and perform live with a band, lonely and socially anxious Hitori "Bocchi" Gotou devotes her time to playing the guitar. On a fateful day, Bocchi meets the outgoing drummer Nijika Ijichi, who invites her to join Kessoku Band when their guitarist, Ikuyo Kita, flees before their first show. Soon after, Bocchi meets her final bandmate—the cool bassist Ryou Yamada.

Although their first performance together is subpar, the girls feel empowered by their shared love for music, and they are soon rejoined by Kita. Finding happiness in performing, Bocchi and her bandmates put their hearts into improving as musicians while making the most of their fleeting high school days.

Comment:

Bocchi the Hero! is quite probably the finest manga adaptation I've ever seen. It was clearly made with enthusiasm and affection, not only for the original material but for music in general. I'd characterize this program as having a base similar to K-On!, combined with the comedy and production style of The Amazing World of Gumball; am I crazy? It's incredibly humorous and relevant, and it all works for me, coupled with a variety of visual styles that complement Bocchi's "episodes" of worry. Kessoku Band's Journey is one to watch every week since it is so much fun to see.
