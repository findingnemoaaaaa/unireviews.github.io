Home Alone


The McCallister family is preparing to spend Christmas in Paris, with Kevin, their youngest son, being ridiculed by his older siblings and cousins. Kevin accidentally ruins the family dinner and is left behind, despite having rented vans to take them to the airport. The family is stalked by the "Wet Bandits," Harry and Marv, who have been breaking into other vacant houses in the neighborhood. Kevin tricks them into thinking his family is still home, forcing them to postpone their plans to rob the McCallister house. Kate manages to find a flight back to Scranton, Pennsylvania, and Gus Polinski offers to let her travel with them to Chicago in a moving van.

On Christmas Eve, Kevin overhears Harry and Marv discussing plans to break into the house, and Kevin suggests reconciling with his son. They break into the house, causing injuries. Kevin calls the police and lures them into a vacant neighboring house, where they ambush Kevin and prepare to get revenge. Marley intervenes and knocks them out with his snow shovel. The police arrest Harry and Marv, identifying all the houses they broke into due to their habit of flooding them.

On Christmas Day, Kevin is disappointed to find his family is still gone, but Kate arrives home and they reconcile. The rest of the family returns to Paris, waiting for a direct flight to Chicago.

Comment:

"Home Alone" features slapstick humor, clever pranks, and witty dialogue. Kevin's inventive booby traps and Harry and Marv's interactions add humor. The film also touches on family, friendship, and the true meaning of Christmas, delivering a heartwarming message.
