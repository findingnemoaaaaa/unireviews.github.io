Insidious: Chapter 3


In 2007, teenager Quinn Brenner meets retired demonologist Elise Rainier, who reluctantly agrees to contact her mother's spirit, Lily, who died a year earlier. Elise warns her not to contact her mother again after sensing a malevolent force. Quinn witnesses a mysterious figure walking away from her apartment building, and she is hit by a car. She is stuck in her home with her father Sean and brother Alex, experiencing paranormal phenomena, including visions of the "Man Who Can't Breathe" wearing an oxygen mask. Sean learns that Grace has passed away, and Elise, who is also grieving after her husband Jack's death, suggests she help his daughter.

JDuring their investigation, Quinn becomes briefly possessed by the "Man Who Can't Breathe" and attacks Sean, Specs, and Tucker. Elise decides to enter the spiritual world and enlists Specs and Tucker to help. During the struggle, Quinn appears, and Elise and the "Man Who Can't Breathe" engage in a struggle. Lily, who left a letter for Quinn, helps her defeat the "Man Who Can't Breathe" by inspiring her to stand up for herself. Elise leaves with Specs and Tucker, and the three form a partnership. Later, Elise's dog barks at an unseen force, and a demon with a red face watches.

Comment:

"Insidious: Chapter 3" maintains the atmospheric and chilling tone of previous films, featuring Leigh Whannell's signature dread and unease. The film uses suspense, psychological horror, and jump scares, while a haunting musical score adds to the atmosphere. The visual style and practical effects effectively build tension and create memorable moments.
