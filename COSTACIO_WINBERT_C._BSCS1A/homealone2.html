Home Alone 2: Lost in New York


The McCallister family prepares to spend Christmas in Miami, but Kevin, their youngest son, views Florida as contradictory to Christmas due to its tropical climate and lack of Christmas trees. At a school Christmas pageant, Kevin retaliates by pushing Buzz, ruining the choir. Kevin apologizes to his family, but refuses to apologize and storms off to the attic. The next morning, Kevin is separated from his family and accidentally boards a flight to New York City. He visits a toy store, where Mr. Duncan plans to donate the proceeds to a children's hospital.

Kevin makes a donation and receives a pair of ceramic turtledoves as a gesture of eternal friendship. The Wet Bandits arrive, and Kevin escapes the hotel. Later, he befriends a pigeon lady in Central Park and decides to prevent a toy store robbery. Kevin rigs the townhouse with booby traps, catches Harry and Marv, and breaks the front window. He evades them, calls the police, and flees to Central Park.

The pigeon lady catches him, but he is saved by a bucket of birdseed, attracting a flock of pigeons and incapacitating them. The family arrives in New York, and Kevin reconciles with his family and gives one of his turtledoves to the pigeon lady, cementing their friendship.

Comment:

"Home Alone 2" is a sequel to the popular comedy, but critics argue it follows the formula, making it predictable and repetitive. Despite this, it delivers laughs and holiday cheer, making it a delightful continuation of Kevin McCallister's hilarious and adventurous journey.
