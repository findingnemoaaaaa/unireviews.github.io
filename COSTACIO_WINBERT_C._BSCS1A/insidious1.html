Insidious


Josh and Renai Lambert move into a new home with their sons Dalton and Foster, and their infant daughter Cali. Dalton slips into a coma and is taken back home, leading to paranormal events. Renai witnesses a bloody handprint on Dalton's bed and a apparition attacking her. The Lamberts move, believing the house to be haunted. Renai witnesses a ghost of a young child dressed in period attire, and Josh's mother Lorraine experiences a nightmare about a demon with a red face in Dalton's bedroom.

Elise Rainier and her team, including psychic Elise Rainier, help Josh project his consciousness into the purgatory dimension called "The Further," inhabited by tortured spirits of the deceased. Josh accuses Elise of being a scam artist but later discovers drawings in Dalton's room that confirm her theory. Elise helps Josh overcome his fear and frees him from the demon. The spirits of The Further invade the real world, terrorizing Renai, Elise, and others. Josh is confronted by the old woman who tormented him as a child and tries to overcome her fear. The family celebrates their victory, but Elise becomes angry when she takes a photo of Josh, revealing that he is now possessed by the ghost of the old woman from his childhood.

Comment:

"Insidious" excels in atmosphere and tension-building techniques, using James Wan's expertise in horror to create a haunting atmosphere. The eerie musical score by Joseph Bishara adds to the dread. The film explores astral projection and a dark, malevolent realm, introducing unique supernatural concepts that keep the audience engaged and invested in the unfolding events.
